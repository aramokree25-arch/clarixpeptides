Step 1

Receiving & Handling

Upon receipt of your lyophilised peptide compounds, follow these three protocols before commencing any research activity to ensure compound integrity is maintained.

01

Visual Inspection

Upon receipt, inspect the vial carefully. Lyophilised peptides may appear as a fine white powder, a translucent film, or may be effectively invisible inside the vial. The absence of visible material does not indicate a defective or empty product � this is entirely normal behaviour for high-purity lyophilised compounds.

02

Temperature Equilibration

Allow cold-shipped vials to reach ambient room temperature fully before opening. Opening a cold vial in a warm environment causes condensation to form on and inside the vial, which can cause peptide degradation and moisture-induced aggregation. Allow at least 30 minutes at room temperature before proceeding.

03

Minimise Exposure

Open vials only in a clean, dry environment. Reseal immediately after each handling session to limit exposure to atmospheric humidity and oxygen � both accelerate peptide chain degradation. When possible, work under a laminar flow hood or biological safety cabinet using sterile technique throughout.

Step 2

Reconstituting Your Peptide

Reconstitution converts a lyophilised (freeze-dried) peptide back into a liquid solution for research use. Follow these three steps in order to preserve compound purity and bioactivity.

1

Solvent Selection

Choosing the correct solvent is the most critical step in reconstitution. Always start with the mildest effective solvent and progress to stronger options only if dissolution is incomplete. Bacteriostatic Water (BW) is the standard choice for most research peptides � its 0.9% benzyl alcohol content inhibits microbial growth and substantially extends the usable shelf life of your reconstituted solution.

Bacteriostatic Water � recommended first choice Sterile distilled water � suitable for short-term use 0.1% Acetic acid � for difficult-to-dissolve peptides Ammonium bicarbonate � if acetic acid is insufficient DMSO / Ethanol � hydrophobic peptides only
Bacteriostatic water extends reconstituted peptide shelf life to 4�8 weeks at 4�C, compared to just 7�10 days with plain sterile water. For any peptide you plan to use over multiple sessions, bacteriostatic water is strongly preferred.
2

Gentle Mixing Technique

Using a sterile insulin syringe, introduce the reconstitution solvent slowly by directing it along the inside glass wall of the vial � never inject it directly onto the lyophilised peptide. Slow side-wall addition prevents foaming and avoids mechanically disrupting the peptide structure.

Once the solvent is added, gently swirl the vial using a slow rolling motion between the palms. Do not vortex or shake vigorously. Mechanical agitation can denature sensitive peptide structures, produce irreversible aggregates, and significantly reduce bioactivity.

A 1 mL sterile insulin syringe is ideal for adding reconstitution solvent � it provides precise volume control and allows you to add the solvent slowly along the vial wall. Use a fresh, sterile syringe for each reconstitution.
3

Dissolution & Clarity

After adding the solvent and gently swirling, allow the vial to stand at room temperature for 15�30 minutes, swirling gently every few minutes. The majority of peptides will dissolve fully within this window to produce a clear, colourless or very slightly tinted solution.

If particles remain after 30 minutes, apply brief, gentle sonication in a laboratory water bath sonicator (30�60 seconds). If the peptide still does not dissolve fully, this may indicate a solvent incompatibility � return to Step 1 and trial an alternative solvent system such as 0.1% acetic acid.

Slight cloudiness immediately after solvent addition is normal and typically resolves within a few minutes of gentle swirling. A solution that remains turbid after 30 minutes of swirling warrants investigation of the solvent choice.

Best Practice

General Research Tips

Additional guidance to protect the integrity of your peptide compounds across every stage of the research lifecycle.

Protect from Light

Many peptides are photosensitive. Store in amber vials or wrap clear vials in aluminium foil. Minimise direct light exposure during handling and reconstitution � UV and even fluorescent light can cleave peptide bonds and accelerate oxidative degradation.

Label Everything Precisely

Label all vials and aliquots with: compound name, concentration (mcg/mL), preparation date, reconstitution solvent, and storage conditions. Ambiguous or missing labels are a leading cause of research protocol errors and wasted compounds.

Avoid Freeze-Thaw Cycling

Repeated freezing and thawing is one of the primary causes of peptide degradation. Pre-aliquot all reconstituted peptides into single-use volumes before placing in the freezer. Once thawed, keep at 4�C and use within the specified refrigerated window.

Use Sterile Technique Throughout

Always use sterile, single-use syringes and needles. Wipe vial septa with 70% isopropyl alcohol before each needle insertion. Work in a clean environment and wear nitrile gloves to prevent sample contamination and cross-contamination between compounds.

Verify Purity with Your COA

All Clarix Peptides products ship with a Certificate of Analysis (COA) confirming =98% purity by HPLC and MS verification. Retain your COA records � they are required for compliant laboratory documentation and audit trails.
